Today I will address one of the sensitive things that are still being followed in few software companies - "Asking the previous employer payslips before sending offer letter"

There are 2 reasons for asking it.
  • To verify if the candidate is not faking experience.
  • To determine their hike.

I'm totally OK with the first one.

But the second reason separates "Great" companies from "OKish" companies.

Let me explain.

Companies already have certain baselines and slabs of CTC predefined for a given role. I understand negotiation is an art. But deciding the starting CTC based on previous employer salary is something we should reconsider whether to continue doing or not.

When you ask a selected candidate about his/her expectation, most candidates will say "Industry standard". This is because they value the company overall and believe that the standards are high and on par with the market.

After this, the moment previous employer payslips are asked, it could damage the reputation a bit.

Just a thought.
I'm just trying to express the impact of this sensitive thing.

5 statements used instead of "You are selected" in the interview.

  • Next round will be with Hiring manager.
  • I will need a few documents from you.
  • We will call you next week for CTC discussion.
  • Are you fine working with XYZ technologies in our team ?
