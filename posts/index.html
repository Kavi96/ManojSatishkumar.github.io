Asking for pay slips
Today I will address one of the sensitive things that are still being followed in few software companies - "Asking the previous employer payslips before sending offer letter"

There are 2 reasons for asking it.
  • To verify if the candidate is not faking experience.
  • To determine their hike.

I'm totally OK with the first one.

But the second reason separates "Great" companies from "OKish" companies.

Let me explain.

Companies already have certain baselines and slabs of CTC predefined for a given role. I understand negotiation is an art. But deciding the starting CTC based on previous employer salary is something we should reconsider whether to continue doing or not.

When you ask a selected candidate about his/her expectation, most candidates will say "Industry standard". This is because they value the company overall and believe that the standards are high and on par with the market.

After this, the moment previous employer payslips are asked, it could damage the reputation a bit.

Just a thought.
I'm just trying to express the impact of this sensitive thing.

3 tips for #zoom interview
With most companies moving to WFH model, interviews over video conferencing have become the only option lately. The world has changed and we software developers love "change" because we have been told that's the only "constant" in our career.

Below are 3 tips you must keep in mind before the interview starts. I assume you have prepared well before this day.

#1. Join the call on time and turn the webcam ON.
- By turning the webcam ON, you get immediately noticed as most candidates and interviewers will not turn it on. They will focus on you a bit more.

#2. Keep your favorite code editor handy.
- Few companies will prefer an online editor. But many times, the interviewer may ask you to open in your local machine due to VPN issues. Don't waste time installing it. Keep it ready.

#3. Keep a sample project ready.
- The last thing you want is getting stuck in setting up a sample project. Don't waste time installing dependencies. Keep the sample project ready where you can quickly code and show the output. If you are in a react.js interview, keep a" hello world" app ready with index.js
